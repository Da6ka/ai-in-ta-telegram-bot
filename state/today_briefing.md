# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 15 July 2026

## AI in Recruitment — What's New

- Candidates are increasingly using AI to cheat live interviews and coding screens, and employers are reacting fast: Bloomberg reports firms including Google, Cisco and McKinsey are reinstating in-person rounds, while Anthropic has had to rewrite its own technical questions because so many applicants were answering with Claude — a signal to rebuild assessments around verifiable, supervised or explicitly AI-permitted work rather than unmonitored take-homes. ([AI Tools Can Help Job Hunters Cheat on Interviews and Coding Tests](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/ai-tools-can-help-job-hunters-cheat-on-interviews-and-coding-tests)) (14 July)
- Fusemachines (NASDAQ: FUSE) launched an Agentic AI Suite for talent acquisition spanning the hiring lifecycle, including a Candidate Shortlisting Agent and a Candidate Trust Agent that surfaces inconsistencies across resumes, applications and screening calls to flag identity-verification needs. As agentic suites proliferate, pressure-test the shortlisting logic for auditability and adverse-impact monitoring before letting it gate candidates. ([Fusemachines Announces Agentic AI Suite for Talent Acquisition](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/07/14/3326853/0/en/Fusemachines-Announces-Agentic-AI-Suite-for-Talent-Acquisition-Bringing-Agentic-AI-Across-the-Hiring-Lifecycle.html)) (14 July)

**Bottom line:** Candidate-side AI is eroding the signal in interviews and screens just as vendors ship agentic "trust" and shortlisting agents to compensate — TA leaders should redesign assessments for an AI-saturated funnel and demand auditable, defensible logic from any agent they deploy to police it.
