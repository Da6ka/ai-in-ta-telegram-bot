# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 21 July 2026

## AI in Recruitment — What's New
- Twenty-six Meta employees have filed what plaintiffs' lawyers call the first AI-driven layoff lawsuit, alleging Meta used AI performance rankings, keystroke and activity monitoring, and token-usage dashboards to pick layoff targets — scores that "by design" can't be accrued by workers on protected medical, pregnancy, or caregiving leave, in claimed violation of the FMLA, ADA, and Pregnant Workers Fairness Act. Meta says humans, not AI, made the decisions; for TA and workforce leaders it's a warning that AI-assisted workforce-selection tools carry the same adverse-impact exposure as hiring screens, and need documented human review and disparate-impact testing before they touch any employment decision. ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/15/meta-workers-sue-over-ai-layoff-math/)) (15 July)

**Bottom line:** The Meta suit drags AI adverse-impact risk out of the hiring funnel and into every automated people-decision — audit where AI scores gate outcomes across your employee lifecycle, not just at the point of hire.
