# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 24 July 2026

## Claude & Anthropic in TA
- Anthropic launched an **Anthropic Economic Index connector** that lets anyone query its labor-usage dataset directly inside Claude — asking which occupations and tasks lean most on AI, drawn from real usage data — and detailed the research agenda for its **Economic Futures Research Fund** on AI's workforce impact. For TA and workforce planners, it's a free way to pressure-test which roles in your org are most AI-exposed before writing next cycle's reqs or reskilling plans. ([The Anthropic Economic Index connector](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-economic-index-connector)) (22 July 2026)

## AI in Recruitment — What's New
- **CJ Olive Young added an "AI task" round to its experienced-developer hiring**, using Krafton's Cofa-Probe solution to score not just candidates' deliverables but how they define the problem, write prompts, iterate, and verify results. It's an early, concrete example of assessment design shifting to measure *AI-collaboration* skill rather than raw output — worth auditing whether your own technical screens still reward working without AI. ([Olive Young Adds AI Task Round in Developer Hiring Drive](https://en.sedaily.com/finance/2026/07/21/olive-young-adds-ai-task-round-in-developer-hiring-drive)) (21 July 2026)

**Bottom line:** Both the data and the tests are moving toward AI-augmented work — map which of your roles are most AI-exposed, then update how you assess for AI fluency before the next hiring cycle.
