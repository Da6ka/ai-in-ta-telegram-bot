# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 4 July 2026

## Claude & Anthropic in TA
- **Anthropic launched Claude Science, a version of Claude tuned for scientific labs and pharma R&D** — its clearest move yet toward purpose-built, vertical Claude applications. For life-sciences TA teams it shifts the skills profile worth sourcing for; for everyone else it previews the playbook a future HR/TA-tuned Claude would likely follow. ([STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-release-claude-science-ceo-dario-amodei/)) (30 June)
- **Anthropic restored access to Claude Fable 5 (and Mythos 5) after adding new cybersecurity classifiers**, following conversations with the US government. It's a reminder that model availability and guardrails move fast — and directly bound what agentic sourcing and screening features the vendors on your stack can ship. ([9to5Google](https://9to5google.com/2026/07/01/anthropic-fable-5-returns-to-claude/)) (1 July)

## AI in Recruitment — What's New
- **The Council of the EU gave final sign-off to the AI Act "omnibus" simplification package, deferring the high-risk compliance deadline — which covers AI used in recruitment, candidate evaluation and selection — from 2 August 2026 to 2 December 2027.** TA teams running AI hiring tools in the EU gain roughly 16 months, but the extension is runway to finish bias testing, human-oversight documentation and vendor due diligence, not a reason to pause them. ([Gibson Dunn](https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/)) (29 June)

**Bottom line:** With the EU's high-risk clock reset to December 2027 and Anthropic shipping purpose-built vertical Claude products, use the reprieve to lock down AI-hiring governance now — and watch whether an HR-tuned Claude is the next vertical to land.
