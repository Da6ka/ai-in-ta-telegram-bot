# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 2 July 2026

## AI in Recruitment — What's New

- **A federal judge let the Mobley v. Workday AI-bias suit proceed**, with Judge Rita Lin's 22 June order allowing FEHA and ADA claims to move toward trial and treating the vendor as an "agent" of employers — meaning your ATS vendor's flaws can become your liability. TA leaders should inventory every AI screening tool, demand bias-audit documentation, and lock in human-review checkpoints now. ([SHRM](https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/news/technology/workday-ai-lawsuit-wake-up-call-hr)) (22 June)
- **HireVue launched its voice-based AI Interviewer**, running two-way conversational screens 24/7 and scoring candidates against IO-validated rubrics — its first product since the March Hireguide acquisition. It pushes automated evaluation earlier in the funnel, so weigh candidate experience and audit trails before deploying at scale. ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hirevue-ai-interviewer-sets-new-enterprise-standard-grounded-in-validated-hiring-science-302803800.html)) (18 June)
- **HR tech funding stayed active into late June**, with HR software firm HR Duo among nine UK rounds totalling £166.65m in the week of 22–26 June, a 49% week-on-week jump — a signal that capital is still flowing to workforce tooling even as investors favour proven scale over moonshots. ([UKTN](https://www.uktech.news/funding/uk-tech-funding-roundup-partly-hr-duo-20260626)) (26 June)

**Bottom line:** With courts now treating AI screening vendors as your agents just as voice-based AI interviewing goes mainstream, the safest move this quarter is a documented audit of every automated tool touching a hiring decision — before adding another.
