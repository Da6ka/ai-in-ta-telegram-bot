# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 3 July 2026

## Claude & Anthropic in TA
- Anthropic's new Economic Index "Cadences" report finds ~49% of jobs now have at least a quarter of their tasks performed with Claude, and — notably — the workers who delegate most to AI report the *most* optimism about pay, security and job mobility. TA leaders should read this as a workforce-planning signal: adoption depth, not headcount, is becoming the differentiator worth screening and reskilling for. ([Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-june-2026-report)) (26 June)

## AI in Recruitment — What's New
- A federal judge (Rita Lin, N.D. Cal.) denied Workday's motion to dismiss, letting FEHA and ADA claims proceed in the Mobley/Hughes AI-bias case — with the court accepting that a vendor whose tool screens, ranks or rejects applicants can act as the employer's agent. Any TA team relying on third-party AI screening should confirm vendor bias-audit documentation and its own liability exposure now, not after discovery. ([HR Dive](https://www.hrdive.com/news/workday-california-AI-bias-lawsuit-feha/823555/)) (22 June)
- Korn Ferry agreed to acquire UK-based RPO giant AMS from OMERS for ~$1.1 billion, creating a 16,000-person talent-solutions firm spanning RPO, campus/early-careers and contingent workforce. Expect accelerated AI-enabled RPO bundling — a prompt for TA leaders to reassess build-vs-outsource as the vendor landscape consolidates. ([Korn Ferry](https://www.kornferry.com/about-us/press/korn-ferry-announces-definitive-agreement-to-acquire-ams)) (29 June)

## Worth Reading
- Forbes contributor Aparna Rae's "AI Didn't Break Hiring. It Scaled the Bias We Already Chose" is a sharp counterweight to vendor optimism, arguing AI screening industrializes existing human bias rather than removing it — useful framing as the Workday ruling raises the stakes on auditability. ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/aparnarae/2026/06/29/ai-didnt-break-hiring-it-scaled-the-bias-we-already-chose/)) (29 June)

**Bottom line:** With courts now treating AI screening vendors as employer agents, the smart move this week is to demand bias-audit evidence from every AI tool in your stack before the market consolidates the choice away from you.
