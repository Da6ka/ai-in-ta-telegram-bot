# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 17 July 2026

## Claude & Anthropic in TA
- Anthropic and Blackstone helped launch **Ode**, a new AI-implementation company backed by roughly $1.5B (with Hellman & Friedman, Goldman Sachs and others), betting that helping enterprises actually deploy models — not the models themselves — is the next trillion-dollar category. It's another heavily capitalized services firm building delivery teams around frontier models like Claude, so expect "AI implementation" and forward-deployed engineer roles to keep hardening into a distinct, in-demand hiring category. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/anthropic-blackstone-bet-the-next-trillion-dollar-ai-business-is-implementation-not-models/)) (15 July 2026)

## AI in Recruitment — What's New
- **AI now shows up in job titles across every sector, not just tech:** HR Dive reports the share of postings referencing AI in the title has more than tripled since 2022, spreading into non-tech fields, alongside Cognizant/Pearson findings that 94% of HR leaders expect AI to create genuinely new entry-level roles. Start rewriting reqs and early-career pipelines around AI-augmented work now, rather than bolting "AI skills" onto legacy job descriptions. ([HR Dive](https://www.hrdive.com/news/more-job-titles-include-ai-across-every-sector/825038/)) (13 July 2026)
- **uRecruits launched version 2.0, explicitly positioning "a human in every decision" as its answer to tightening AI hiring laws** — AI scores and organizes candidates across screening and matching, but a recruiter makes each call. Whether or not you use the tool, it's a signal that "human-in-the-loop by design" is becoming a compliance selling point; press your own vendors on where the human review actually sits. ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/as-ai-hiring-laws-tighten-urecruits-launches-2-0-with-a-human-in-every-decision-302824964.html)) (14 July 2026)

**Bottom line:** With capital pouring into AI *deployment* and "AI-touched" roles spreading across every sector, the TA priority this week is pipeline design — rewrite entry-level reqs around AI-augmented judgment and confirm exactly where human review lives in every screening tool you run.
