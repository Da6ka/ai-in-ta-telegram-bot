# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 20 July 2026

## AI in Recruitment — What's New

- Upwork's **Future Workforce Index 2026** found that more than one in three skilled US knowledge workers now freelance (up from roughly one in four a year ago), and freelancers doing AI work earn about 34% more per hour than those who don't — a signal to build contingent and blended-workforce channels into your hiring plan, not just full-time reqs. ([Upwork's Future Workforce Index 2026](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/07/14/3326964/0/en/Upwork-s-Future-Workforce-Index-2026-How-AI-is-Redefining-the-Value-of-Work-as-Skilled-Freelancing-Accelerates.html)) (14 July 2026)
- Guthrie AI raised a **$4M seed led by Chicago Ventures** to put an AI "Virtual Bid Assistant" on every glazing team — notable less for the construction vertical than for how it markets agents as staff you "hire, train, and deploy," a framing spreading fast as TA teams begin onboarding AI agents alongside people. ([Guthrie AI Raises $4 Million Seed Round](https://venturebeat.com/business/guthrie-ai-raises-4-million-seed-round-led-by-chicago-ventures-to-put-a-virtual-bid-assistant-on-every-glazing-team)) (13 July 2026)

**Bottom line:** Both items point to a workforce where the next "hire" is increasingly a freelancer or an AI agent rather than a full-time employee — extend your sourcing pipelines, budgets, and governance to cover contingent and agent labor now, before those channels outgrow your traditional req process.
