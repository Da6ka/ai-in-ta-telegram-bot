# Daily AI Recruitment Briefing — 3 July 2026

## Claude & Anthropic in TA
- Anthropic shipped **Claude Sonnet 5** as the new default for Free and Pro users — near-Opus agentic performance (planning, tool use, autonomous multi-step work) at introductory pricing of $2/$10 per million input/output tokens through 31 August. For TA teams, cheaper and more capable agents make it materially less expensive to run sourcing, screening, and candidate-comms workflows on Claude. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-launches-claude-sonnet-5-as-a-cheaper-way-to-run-agents/)) (30 June 2026)

## AI in Recruitment — What's New
- Recruiting is becoming "a battle between AI and AI": candidate-side tools now inflate résumés to the point that nearly every applicant looks like a perfect fit, fueling a rise in "skillfishing" — exaggerated skills that collapse once someone is hired. The practical takeaway for TA leaders is to shift screening toward verification and structured, evidence-based assessment rather than paper credentials. ([HR Dive](https://www.hrdive.com/news/what-happens-when-candidates-overuse-ai-what-recruiters-can-do/824157/)) (July 2026)

## Worth Reading
- Forbes' Aparna Rae argues AI didn't break hiring so much as **scale the biases organizations already chose** — a sharp frame for TA leaders auditing where automated screening quietly amplifies existing (and sometimes discriminatory) selection criteria. ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/aparnarae/2026/06/29/ai-didnt-break-hiring-it-scaled-the-bias-we-already-chose/)) (29 June 2026)

**Bottom line:** As Anthropic drives the cost of capable agents down, the real constraint on AI in hiring is no longer capability or budget but trust — audit your own screening criteria and build verification into the process before you scale automation.
