# Agentic recruiting

AI-powered agents are automating recruitment workflows end-to-end: sourcing, screening, interview coordination, and candidate communication. Unlike chatbots that draft text, recruiting agents actively interact with hiring systems (ATSs, scheduling tools, communication platforms), make candidate-facing decisions (subject to human review), and run 24/7. The defining 2026 shift is from isolated point automation to orchestrated, multi-stage agentic workflows that free recruiters for judgment-heavy work. Josh Bersin's mid-year review confirms agents have "arrived" — Eightfold, ClearCo, Fusemachines, and others are shipping full suites in general availability, with early adopters reporting time-to-hire drops from two weeks to three days. International adoption is accelerating: Xobin's NURA agent is already deployed at major Indian IT services and banking firms, processing a million résumés in ten minutes. The risk is real: agent-driven screening, evaluation, and scheduling decisions are now live in production; pressure-test auditability and adverse impact before deployment.

## Open questions

- How do multi-agent systems distribute decision authority between agents, between agents and humans, and across orgs? Who is liable when an agent makes an adverse-impact decision?
- What audit trails and bias-testing approaches actually scale to agentic systems with feedback loops?
- How do recruiting agents handle edge cases — unusual backgrounds, career pivots, protected-class scenarios — without built-in bias from their training data?
