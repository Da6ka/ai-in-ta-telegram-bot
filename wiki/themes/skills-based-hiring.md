# Skills-based hiring

Resumes and credentials are losing signal. AI screening is pushing organizations away from degree-first hiring toward demonstrated ability — structured assessments, portfolios, take-homes, and live problem-solving. The shift is both accelerated by AI (which can parse a portfolio as easily as a resume) and enabled by it (agentic tools make skills verification cheaper and faster to run at scale). Industry adoption is rising fast: Dice reports 73% of US tech postings now mention AI skills, and leading companies like Palo Alto Networks are hiring almost exclusively via assessment and hackathons, not interviews.

## Open questions

- Which "skills" are actually job-predictive, and which are just current-fad proxies that will fade as AI fluency becomes baseline?
- How do you assess AI-augmented judgment in practice (i.e., the ability to prompt well and interpret model output), rather than just testing raw AI knowledge?
- Do assessments designed around demonstrated ability scale to high-volume hiring without ballooning recruiter workload?
